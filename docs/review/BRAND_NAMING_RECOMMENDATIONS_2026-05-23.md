# Brand Naming Recommendations

Date: 2026-05-23
Repo: /Users/pranay/Projects/LLM/image/waste_seg/waste_segregation_app

## Bottom line

The user selected ReLoop.
That is now the canonical brand for the product.

"Waste Segregation App" was never a real brand. It was only a literal descriptor. It is too narrow for the product that now spans:
- AI waste classification
- disposal guidance
- daily free scans + premium unlocks
- token wallet and abuse control
- impact tracking
- achievements, leaderboard, family and community loops
- public acquisition / SEO surface

## Recommended brand architecture

- Brand: ReLoop
- Descriptor: AI waste sorting and disposal guidance

Example:
- ReLoop
- AI waste sorting, disposal guidance, and impact tracking

## Why ReLoop won

- Broad enough for the full product surface, not just classification
- Works for circular economy positioning and the gamified habit loop
- Feels ownable and product-like instead of bureaucratic
- Can cover AI guidance, community, and impact tracking without boxing the product in

## Alternatives that were considered

These should remain documented as fallback options and naming references, even though they are not the canonical brand.

### 1) BinWise
Best if you want clarity first.

Pros:
- Immediately understandable
- Friendly and consumer-oriented
- Fits waste classification, disposal advice, and daily use

Cons:
- More narrowly tied to bins and sorting
- Less expansive than ReLoop for broader community, impact, and partnership features

### 2) CycleWise
Best if you want a more serious, durable brand.

Pros:
- Ties naturally to recycling and circularity
- Feels mature and platform-like
- Broad enough to cover future partner and community features

Cons:
- Slightly less immediate than BinWise
- Can feel a bit enterprise if the visual identity is not handled well

### 3) WasteWise
Best as a safe fallback.

Pros:
- Clear and direct
- Easy to understand quickly
- Historically present in the repo and legacy references

Cons:
- Still generic
- Still centered on waste as a noun, not the product experience or habit loop
- Less brandable than ReLoop or CycleWise

### 4) SortWise
Best if you want to emphasize the core AI action.

Pros:
- Points to the classification workflow
- Simple and clean
- Slightly more modern than a literal app descriptor

Cons:
- Still pretty descriptive
- Less emotionally resonant than ReLoop

### 5) GreenLoop
Best if you want a broader eco brand.

Pros:
- Broad and flexible
- Can extend into education, community, and impact tracking

Cons:
- Generic
- Could drift too far from the core waste use case

## What not to use

- Literal feature-list names as the brand
- Names that lock the product into only one feature when the roadmap is broader
- Names that are too cute or abstract to trust in a utility product
- "Waste Segregation App" as the public brand

## Suggested descriptor layer

Keep the brand and the explanation separate:

1. Brand name
   - ReLoop

2. Descriptor / subtitle
   - AI waste sorting and disposal guidance
   - Daily scans, premium unlocks, and community impact

That gives:
- a real brand for marketing
- a clear explanation for first-time users
- room to expand into premium, family, and community features

## Practical migration note

The repo should now standardize on ReLoop for visible brand references.
Legacy support addresses, legal URLs, or operational mailbox names should be migrated separately if and when the mailbox/domain is changed. Do not invent new contact details without verifying them.
