# User Research & Usability Testing Methodology

**Decision it unblocks**: How product decisions are validated — whether through structured user research, or through team intuition / A/B tests alone.

**Key questions**:
- Research cadence: how often do we talk to users? Weekly (continuous discovery), monthly, quarterly?
- Methodology mix: moderated usability tests, unmoderated sessions, intercept surveys, diary studies, tree-jacks?
- Participant sourcing: how to recruit users (in-app prompt, email list, social, existing community)?
- What's the minimum viable research practice for a 1-2 person team? (4 user interviews per quarter with structured synthesis?)
- How are research findings documented and shared? (One-pagers, video clips, tagged in the exploration map?)
- A/B test interpretation: what's the process for turning an experiment result into a product decision?
- NPS / CSAT / CES tracking: do we measure satisfaction systematically, and how does it feed the roadmap?
- Competitive UX benchmarking: regular teardowns of comparable scan-centric apps (Yuka, PictureThis, etc.)?
- Design validation before building: prototype testing with tools like Figma prototypes or Flutter widget screenshots?
- User segmentation in research: do we intentionally recruit across motivation archetypes, experience levels, and demographics?

**Kill criteria**:
- Team of 1 cannot sustain regular user research without impacting delivery — but skipping research entirely leads to building wrong things.
- Minimum viable: structured interview protocol for every major feature before building, plus quarterly synthesis.
